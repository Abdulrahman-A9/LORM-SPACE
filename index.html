Space flights

Apollo 11

Year: 1969

Operator: NASA

The first crewed mission to land on the Moon. Astronauts Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin became the first and second humans to walk on the lunar surface on July 20, 1969.

Space flights

Space Shuttle

Years: 1981-2011

Operator: NASA

A program that enabled reusable spacecraft to transport astronauts and cargo to and from space. It played a critical role in constructing the International Space Station (ISS) and deploying the Hubble Space Telescope.

Space flights

Cassini Huygens

Years: 1997-2017

Operators: NASA and ESA (European Space Agency)

A mission to study Saturn and its moons. Cassini orbited Saturn and collected invaluable data about its atmosphere, rings, and moons. The Huygens probe landed on Titan, Saturn's largest moon.

Space flights

Voyager 1 and 2

Year: 1977

Operator: NASA

Twin spacecraft designed for long-term exploration of the outer planets. Voyager 1 provided detailed images of Jupiter and Saturn, while Voyager 2 explored Uranus and Neptune.

Space flights

Juno

Year: 2011

Operator: NASA

Details: A mission to study Jupiter's atmosphere, magnetic field, and gravitational field. Juno aims to understand the planet's formation and structure, providing insights into the gas giant's role in the solar system.

Hubble Space Telescope

Launch Date: April 24, 1990

Purpose: Astronomy

The Hubble Space Telescope has provided some of the most detailed images of distant galaxies, nebulae, and other astronomical phenomena. It operates in the ultraviolet, visible, and near-infrared spectra.

International Space Station (ISS)

Launch Date: November 20, 1998 (first module)

Purpose: Space research

The ISS serves as a microgravity and space environment research laboratory where scientific research is conducted in astrobiology, astronomy, meteorology, and physics.

GPS (Global Positioning System) Satellites

Launch Date: August 27, 2002 (first satellite)

Purpose: Space exploration

The ISS serves as a microgravity and space environment research laboratory where scientific research is conducted in astrobiology, astronomy, meteorology, and physics.

GOES (Geostationary Operational Environmental Satellites)

Launch Date: First satellite launched in 1975

Purpose: Weather monitoring

GOES satellites provide real-time data on weather conditions, including storm tracking and severe weather alerts, essential for meteorological forecasting.

Landsat Satellites

Launch Date: First satellite launched in 1972

Purpose: Earth observation

The Landsat program has been crucial for land use planning, agriculture monitoring, and environmental management, providing detailed imagery of Earth's surface.

Yuri Gagarin

The first human to travel into space, launched on April 12, 1961, aboard "Vostok 1." He became a global icon of space exploration.

lNeil Armstrong

Sally Ride

The first American woman to travel into space, flying on June 18, 1983, aboard the Space Shuttle Challenger. She is considered a symbol for women in the field of space exploration.
